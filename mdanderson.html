The Ideas Lab

At the MD Anderson IDEAS Lab, an innovation hub focused on surgical and patient care challenges, I collaborated with Krystal Kimiagaree under the direction of Dr. Justin Bird and Mrs. Savannah Esteve Morreale. Our focus was retraction-related discomfort in orthopedic oncology surgeries: surgeons holding retractors for hours at a stretch, and the grip fatigue and ergonomic strain that accumulates over a procedure. I ran 15 clinician sessions and translated that input into 20 design requirements that guided each iteration, covering ergonomics, ease of cleaning, and manufacturability. From there I fabricated SLA and FDM prototypes and silicone cast handles, incorporating surgeon feedback across six CAD revisions to improve grip geometry and reduce cleaning complexity. This experience grounded my design instincts in clinical reality: the constraints surgeons operate under, the tradeoffs between performance and sterilizability, and how much a small change in grip angle or surface texture can matter after three hours in the OR.
