About

Luke Yuen is a first-year Master of Science student in Mechanical Engineering at Stanford University, specializing in robotics. He graduated from Rice University with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering and a minor in Engineering Design. During his time at Rice, Luke co-invented VacuTrac, a patent-pending surgical device that integrates tissue retraction and suction for spinal surgery, earning recognition as a 2025 National Inventors Hall of Fame Collegiate Inventors Competition Finalist. He has also contributed to surgical innovation at the MD Anderson Cancer Center IDEAS Lab and served as a Head Teaching Assistant for Rice's Engineering Design program. With upcoming industry experience at Apple and a foundation in rapid prototyping, hands-on fabrication, and human-centered design, Luke is committed to building thoughtful, functional systems that bridge engineering and real-world impact.
