THEMIS

THEMIS is a wearable blood pressure monitoring device designed to address bias in traditional sensing technologies, particularly for individuals with higher BMIs and darker skin tones. Named after the Greek goddess of fairness and justice, THEMIS reflects our commitment to equitable healthcare through inclusive design. By combining ergonomic hardware with custom-calibrated sensing, the device aims to deliver more accurate, representative data for underrepresented patient populations.

LSCI

LSCI is a laser speckle contrast imaging system designed to visualize real-time blood flow during reconstructive surgeries. Built as part of a multidisciplinary team, I contributed to the mechanical design of the enclosure and mounting system, and helped optimize the optical focal length between the laser and lens for clearer imaging. The system offers surgeons a compact, cost-effective alternative to traditional perfusion monitoring tools.
